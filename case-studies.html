SVAMITVA — Uttar Pradesh Phase I

SVAMITVA
Uttar Pradesh ₹180 Cr 2020-22

Drone-based mapping of 25,000+ inhabited villages across UP, creation of property cards for 18 lakh households, and integration with revenue records. Pioneer project demonstrating drone survey scalability in rural India.

Key Outcomes:
  • 25,000 villages mapped with <2cm GSD drone orthomosaic
  • 18 lakh property cards issued to rural households
  • Land rights digitised and integrated with SVAMITVA portal

Bhopal Smart City Digital Twin

DIGITAL TWIN
Madhya Pradesh ₹84 Cr 2022-24

Comprehensive 3D city model integrating 18 spatial data layers, real-time IoT feeds (traffic, air quality, water supply), and citizen service portal. Backend for Bhopal Smart City operations and urban planning.

Key Outcomes:
  • 18 data layers integrated — spatial + real-time streams
  • 23% improvement in asset management efficiency
  • IoT-enabled predictive maintenance for water supply

DILRMP Telangana — DHARANI Portal

DILRMP
Telangana ₹240 Cr 2020-23

Complete digitisation of Telangana land records including RoR, mutation, and registration. DHARANI portal integration enabling single-window citizen access to land record services and GIS-based property verification.

Key Outcomes:
  • 100% digitisation of all revenue records across state
  • DHARANI portal processes 5,000+ daily transactions
  • Property disputes reduced by 40% via spatial verification

Pune PCMC GIS-Based Property Tax

GIS SURVEY
Maharashtra ₹12.5 Cr 2021-23

Drone survey and GIS-based property tax assessment for Pune Corporation covering 7+ lakh properties. Integration of satellite-derived building footprints with revenue records for accurate tax base estimation.

Key Outcomes:
  • 340% increase in tax collection post-GIS integration
  • Detection of 1.2 lakh missing/unauthorised structures
  • Property disputes reduced through transparent GIS-based assessment

Amaravati Capital City GIS Master Plan

SMART CITY
Andhra Pradesh ₹320 Cr 2016-19

Greenfield city planning using comprehensive GIS including master plan spatial encoding, utility corridor design, and environmental assessment. Landmark example of GIS-first city planning in India.

Key Outcomes:
  • Master plan fully encoded in GIS with 45 spatial layers
  • Utility network design (water, power, waste) optimised via GIS
  • Environmental zoning and green space planning integrated

Integration of cadastral maps from all 33 districts of Gujarat into unified GIS. BISAG-N platform linking land records with property tax, mutation processing, and citizen services.

Key Lessons Learned from Project Portfolio

Data Quality is Non-Negotiable

The most successful projects (Bhopal DT, Telangana DILRMP, Pune PCMC) invested heavily in data quality assurance during acquisition phase. Projects that rushed data collection faced 18-24 month delays in operationalisation due to conflicting boundaries, missing attributes, and user distrust.

Stakeholder Alignment Precedes Technology

SVAMITVA UP's success stemmed from early alignment with village revenue records keepers and gram panchayats. Conversely, early-stage Smart City projects failed due to insufficient coordination between ULB departments. Build governance structures before deploying platforms.

Technology Lock-In Creates Long-Term Costs

Several projects discovered proprietary platform switching costs (15-40% of original investment) when moving to next-generation solutions. Amaravati's master plan GIS faced interoperability challenges migrating from proprietary to open-source. Recommend open standards and APIs from inception.
